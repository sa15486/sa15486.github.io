What is Pond?

Pond Game is an educational game, created to help children to learn more about the lifecycle of tadpoles. More information can be found on tadpoles.org.uk.

Purpose

Our goal was to build a simple ecological model of the pond ecosystem which primary school children can play with to help them understand how ponds work.

The game allows students to model multiple combinations of pond ecosystems with different abiotic and biotic factors and observe the changes in ecosystems overtime. Students should be able to add a range of different organisms to the pond as well as changing the environmental factors such as nutrient content, temperature and sunlight hours/intensity.

The emphasis concerning the simulation is tadpoles. Tadpoles are spawned from their eggs (frogspawn). Overtime the tadpoles will grow into froglets and then on to frogs.

The aim for the students is to try and ensure that most of the tadpoles spawned will survive long enough to grow into frogs. We will include scoring by giving a reward for each tadpole that has survived to become a frog. This should provide encouragement to the students to find the most sustainable ecosystem.
